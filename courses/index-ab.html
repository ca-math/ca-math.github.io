P1.
Find the equation of the tangent line to the curve: \( e^{2y} x + \frac{3}{\pi} \arcsin(x) = 1 \) at \( x=\frac{1}{2}\).
If you don't remember what \( \arcsin(1/2) \) or \( \ln 1 \) are equal to, you need to review or relearn precalculus concepts.
Find the y-value: \( e^{2y} = 1 \rightarrow y=0 \).
Implicitly differentiate with respect to x:
\( \frac{d(LS)}{dx} = \frac{d(RS)}{dx} \rightarrow e^{2y} + x \left(e^{2y}\right) 2\frac{dy}{dx} + \frac{3}{\pi} \frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}} = 0 \).
Plug in the x- and y-values directly into the result above, to find: \( \frac{dy}{dx}|_{(x=\frac{1}{2}, y=0)} = \frac{-2\sqrt{3}-\pi}{\pi} \)
Set up the point-slope formula, then simplify to: \( y =\left( \frac{-2\sqrt{3}-\pi}{\pi}\right)x +\frac{\sqrt{3}}{\pi} +\frac{1}{2}\)
